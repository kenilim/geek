Severe Disability Assessment (SDA)
Provides an assessment of your disability status conducted by a Ministry of Health (MOH)-accredited severe disability assessor.
SERVICE HIGHLIGHTS
  • House-call, Centre-based
  • Fees payable
  • Referral not required
Service Providers
About the service

SDA is required if you are:

  • Applying for long-term care severe disability financial assistance schemes administered by the Agency for Integrated Care (AIC)
    • CareShield Life
    • ElderShield
    • ElderFund
    • Interim Disability Assistance Programme for the Elderly (IDAPE)
    • MediSave Care
  • Applying for supplement schemes of CareShield Life and ElderShield administered by private insurers – Great Eastern, Income and SingLife;
  • Undergoing a disability review to ascertain continued scheme eligibility; or
  • Undergoing an underwriting to enrol into selected schemes.

How to find a severe disability assessor near you:

  • Search via the postal code of your residential address in the Service Providers section; or
  • See a list of island-wide housecall assessors who can do the assessment in the comfort of your home.

You are strongly encouraged to bring along any medical reports or discharge summaries when you go for your SDA appointment.

Who is it for?

For anyone who require an SDA to determine your ability to perform each of the 6 Activities of Daily Living, as certified by an MOH-accredited severe disability assessor.

These 6 activities are:

  • Feeding
  • Dressing
  • Toileting
  • Washing
  • Walking or moving around
  • Transferring from bed to chair and vice versa
What to expect?

Assessments are strictly by appointment only. Please call ahead to make an appointment with the clinic or assessor before going to the clinic.

A typical SDA will last about 45 minutes or longer.

The fees are as follows, if:

  • You visit an assessor’s clinic: $100
  • An assessor visits your house: $250

You will be reimbursed the assessment fee which you have paid if you are assessed to be severely disabled.

For CareShield Life claims, the assessment fee for the first disability assessment will be waived, regardless of whether you are assessed to be severely disabled, or whether the claim is successful.

How to apply?

To find a severe disability assessor located near you, you can:

  • Enter the postal code of your residential address in the Service Providers section; or
  • See a list of island-wide housecall assessors who can do the assessment in the comfort of your home.

You are strongly encouraged to bring along any medical reports or discharge summaries when you go for your SDA appointment.
